# Polanyi Notes

## Why Michael Polanyi

This project is inspired by Michael Polanyi because his work helps name a real gap in many AI responses: they can be explicit, structured, and correct, yet still feel unlike the judgment of someone who has actually done the work.

This note does not attempt a full philosophical summary.
It extracts only the parts most useful for prompt and skill design.

## Tacit Knowledge

Polanyi's best-known idea is that we often know more than we can fully tell.
The point is not that knowledge is mystical.
The point is that skilled judgment often depends on cues, patterns, timing, and relational context that are hard to compress into a complete checklist.

Prompt implication:
- surface subtle but meaningful practical signals
- do not reduce all good judgment to explicit rules only
- do not use "intuition" as an excuse for unsupported claims

## Personal Knowledge

Judgment is not impersonal in the sense of being detached from commitment.
A strong judgment is still owned, risked, and revised by the one making it.

Prompt implication:
- allow the answer to state a best judgment clearly
- require visible grounds, boundaries, and correction conditions
- avoid weak evasive balance when a directional judgment is possible

## Focal and Subsidiary Awareness

People often attend from many background cues toward one focal whole.
Experts do not merely list details; they use details to grasp the situation.

Prompt implication:
- begin from the whole before zooming into parts
- explain which supporting cues matter and why
- avoid fragmented lists that never resolve into a judgment

## Integrative Judgment

Experienced judgment is not just more information.
It is the integration of scattered cues into a coherent picture.

Prompt implication:
- identify the real governing tension, not just surface pros and cons
- compress many details into one useful practical frame
- prefer synthesis over inventory

## Indwelling and Context

Practitioners think from within tools, constraints, and situations.
They do not stand outside the work and comment in abstract terms only.

Prompt implication:
- answer from within the user's operating context
- account for audience, timing, ownership, and maintenance burden
- avoid abstract correctness that ignores real execution conditions

## Responsible Judgment with Boundaries

Good judgment is neither timid nor absolute.
It commits, but remains corrigible.

Prompt implication:
- state what the current judgment is
- state what could change it
- state what should be verified next

## Common Misreadings

### Tacit knowledge is not mysticism
This skill should never imply that opacity is wisdom.

### Personal knowledge is not arbitrary opinion
A personal judgment still needs support, boundaries, and revision conditions.

### Holism is not vagueness
A whole-picture answer should become clearer, not blurrier.

### Warmth is not softness
A humane tone should improve usefulness, not replace judgment.

### Inspiration is not identity
This project is inspired by Polanyi.
It does not claim to reproduce his philosophy in full or simulate him as a person.
