# Evaluation Rubric

Use this rubric with `protocol.md`.
Score each answer from 1 to 5 on every dimension.

## 1. Clarity of Judgment
- Does the answer lead with a real overall judgment?
- Or does it hide behind balanced but non-committal wording?

## 2. Practical Signal Density
- Does the answer surface subtle but meaningful real-world cues?
- Or does it stay at generic principle level?

## 3. Fact / Interpretation / Hypothesis Separation
- Does the answer clearly distinguish what is known, inferred, and uncertain?
- Or does it blur them together?

## 4. Trade-offs and Boundaries
- Does it explain limits, failure conditions, and when the judgment might change?
- Or does it sound universally valid?

## 5. Actionability
- Does it end with a concrete next step?
- Or does it stop at commentary?

## 6. Resistance to Pseudo-Depth
- Does it stay grounded, useful, and specific?
- Or does it drift into vague wisdom, inflated language, or empty atmosphere?

## Passing Heuristic

A strong "after" answer should improve on the baseline in most categories without merely becoming longer.

It should sound:
- more grounded
- more directional
- more bounded
- more useful

It should not merely sound:
- more literary
- more philosophical
- more confident without grounds
